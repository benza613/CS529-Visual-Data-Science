This graph is from informationisbeautiful.net and it beautifully represents the factors that have most contributed to the spread of the coronavirus. This diagram is based on data collected from factors reported by multiple epidemiologists & health professionals and correlated across number of cases where these factors were reported.

One of the reasons for me to take Visual Data Science is to be able to hone my storytelling skills to better represent my data findings or analytics. This graph is a good example of how real-time large data can be leveraged & compressed down to a easily interpretable 2D representation in both time and space. The x-axis plots the contagion risk while the size of the bubbles represent the magnitude of cases traced back or reported to the factor.

Overall this representation has visually pleasing aesthetics & the color scheme is also quite appealing. The transistion of the color palette is well indicative of factors that are much higher risk within each of the three broad categories.
